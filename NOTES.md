# Notes

The model uses purely prosodic, language-agnostic signal from audio strictly before each pause: energy decay/slope into the pause, pitch slope and turn-normalized final pitch (falling vs. level/rising = continuation cue), voicing ratio, final-voiced-run length vs. the turn's average (final-syllable lengthening), speaking rate, and turn-context (pause index, elapsed time, durations of the turn's *already-finished* prior pauses). It pools English and Hindi together with GroupKFold CV (grouped by turn) to pick a GradientBoostingClassifier, deliberately avoiding any ASR/lexical features since the hidden test set is mostly Hindi and text-free prosody should transfer better than anything language-specific. Grouped-CV AUC is ~0.70; the in-sample AUC reported in RUNLOG (~0.93) is optimistic because the shipped model is refit on all 200 turns, so treat ~0.70 as the honest generalization estimate. It still beats the silence-only baseline by a wide margin (700ms vs 1600ms on English, 610ms vs 850ms on Hindi at ≤5% interrupted turns). Where it fails: short/quiet turns with under ~5 voiced frames of context (falls back to near-default features), turns with heavy background noise where the autocorrelation pitch tracker misfires, and genuinely ambiguous trailing-off pauses that even a human listener would hesitate on. With one more day: add a simple VAD-free speaking-rate/rhythm baseline per speaker (normalize features by that speaker's own opening seconds), try a small per-language calibration layer instead of one global threshold, and hand-listen to the ~40-50 misclassified pauses per language to look for a systematic feature gap (e.g. rising-intonation questions being called EOT).
